How these questions affect time & cost

Short version: these aren't paperwork — they are the schedule. A few of them have to be answered before I can write a single line of the results engine, and a couple decide how much gets built (or rebuilt).

  • The build is about 5-6 weeks of my time — but that clock only starts on the results engine once the "code-blocking" items below are decided. Every week they stay open is a week the main work can't move.
  • One question (data residency, #13) picks the entire backend. "Europe is fine" = I start now. "Must be inside Israel" = a different, slower setup. Deciding it late means rebuilding, not adjusting.
  • The writing (Section B) is on your side, not mine. ~30 blocks of result copy + natural Hebrew. The results page can't ship without it. If it lands by ~week 5 we stay on track; if it's late, the build waits on it.
  • Answers are cheap, reversals are expensive. A decision made now is a setting I type in. The same decision made after I've built around the default is paid rework.

My estimate and price assume: the code-blocking answers come back in week 1, residency is decided up front, and content arrives by week 5. If those slip, the timeline slips with them, and big mid-project changes get quoted separately.

C. Product / funnel

We recommend changing the spec — confirm.

  1. Email gate placement.

    Spec puts it before the quiz. We strongly recommend moving it to after completion (capture email to unlock/save the full report, with a teaser shown first). This typically lifts completion substantially. OK to move it?

    In plain termsRight now the spec asks for the person's email before they take the quiz. People hate giving their email before they get anything, so a lot of them quit.     In plain termsThe spec says people can't go back to a previous question. But one accidental tap out of 40 with no way to fix it makes people rage-quit. I want to let them undo the last answer. Still fast, just not a trap. OK?

D. Privacy & legal

Blocks launch, not build.

  1. Email handling.

    Spec says "store hashed." That makes emailing the PDF and showing emails in the admin panel impossible. We'll instead encrypt the email at rest (recoverable) + add a lookup index. Confirm that's acceptable.

    In plain termsThe spec's way of storing emails would scramble them so badly we could never read them again — but we need to read them to email people their PDF and show them in your admin panel. My way keeps them locked and secure but still usable. That's standard and safe. OK to do it my way?

  2. GatingData residency.

    This decides our entire backend platform. Most managed hosts (Supabase/Vercel/Neon) have no Israel region — best is EU/Frankfurt. AWS does have a Tel Aviv region (il-central-1).

    • If EU/Frankfurt is acceptable (true for most GDPR/Israeli-law cases) → we use Supabase (fast to ship). This is our default until you say otherwise.
    • If data must physically live in Israel (hard legal/enterprise requirement) → we switch to AWS RDS Postgres in Tel Aviv from day 1 (more setup, slower MVP).

    Which is it?

    In plain termsThis is the most important question for the price and timeline. Where is the user data legally allowed to be stored? Europe (Frankfurt) is easy, cheap, and fast — and fine for almost every legal case. "It must physically be inside Israel" is possible but means a slower, more expensive setup from day one. Most projects are fine with Europe — but if a lawyer or a big client told you it has to be Israel, tell me now, because changing it later is a rebuild.
