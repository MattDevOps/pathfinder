A developer's honest review — before we build

Hi Rafi — I went through everything you sent in depth: the prototype, the technical brief, and the business plan. Because I'm about to build the full version around it (real time, real money), I went through it like an engineer and a skeptic on purpose — I'd rather catch things now than after months of work. Here's my honest input.

First — the prototype is genuinely good

It's complete, the flow works, the 192 career write-ups are real, and it's a strong demo. You're much further along than most people at this stage. Everything below is me protecting that head start, not doubting it.

Things I caught in the prototype

Worth fixing before launch — these are objective, not opinions.

  1. A hidden bias toward "Builder."

    When someone answers in a balanced or "it depends" way — which a lot of unsure people will — the math ends up tied, and the code quietly picks Builder every time. Builder is exactly who gets pushed toward the most expensive €1,499 tier. So the people least sure about themselves get nudged toward "go start a company." We should fix that.

  2. One of the 12 fields is almost impossible to land on.

    "Craft / Costruzione" barely earns any points from the questions, so users who'd truly belong there get sent somewhere else. It's effectively a dead option right now.

  3. The colors/shapes step doesn't change anyone's result.

    The code ignores it — yet it shows a "high coherence" badge that isn't really measuring anything. We should either make it count for real, or keep it as an honest fun intro and drop the "personality science" claim. (Heads-up: "Lüscher test" and "Psycho-Geometrics" are trademarked names, so I'd avoid naming them.)

  4. The earning-potential stars are very inflated.

    About 62% of all paths are rated 5 out of 5. A sharp user notices everything looks like a jackpot and trusts it less. Worth re-rating honestly.

  5. The documents target two different countries.

    The technical brief is written for Israel (Hebrew, shekels, Israeli privacy law); the business plan is for Italy (Italian, euros). We need to pick one before I build — they need different setups.

The bigger picture — my honest recommendation

Prove people pay before we build the full platform

The riskiest assumption in the whole plan is simple: will people actually pay? The plan assumes about 8 out of every 100 free quiz-takers buy the €99 plan. For a free quiz aimed at young people, a more realistic number is closer to 1 in 100. That single assumption is what everything else — revenue, hiring, the raise — is built on. So before we build the full app, I strongly suggest we prove it cheaply.

And think about who actually pays

The people who feel this pain most (young, still figuring things out) are usually the ones with the least money to spend. The people who'd more readily pay — parents, universities, and the publicly-funded orientation programs (PNRR) — are barely in the plan. That might actually be the easier, bigger business: getting a school or program to pay so their students take it for free. Worth testing alongside everything else.

What I'd do before building the full app

All cheap, all roughly 1-3 weeks — and we can use the prototype you already have.

  1. Smoke-test the paid offer.

    Put the existing prototype online with a real "Buy the €99 plan" button and spend ~€100 driving people to it. The only question that matters: does anyone actually pull out a card? Quiz completions don't count — purchases do.

  2. Sell 5 coaching sessions by hand.

    From your own network, this month. If 5 people pay, the coaching tiers are real. If not, we learn that early and cheaply.

  3. Get one institutional pilot.

    Email a few universities or a PNRR program and ask for one small paid pilot. If even one says yes, that's potentially the real business.

If any of these work, I build with total confidence — and I'll know exactly which version to build. If they don't, we've saved months and a lot of money, and we adjust.

To be clear: I think there's something real here, or I wouldn't be putting this much into it. I just want to point the build at the version we've proven — not the version on paper. Happy to jump on a call and walk through any of this.
